Duo-Synth

Multi-user Music Movement Interaction

Duo-Synth is a personal project in music-movement interaction. I'm researching on expressive ways to communicate sound through movements, using 2 kinect 3D cameras. It aims to create an intuitive storytelling channel for artists to integrate sound art and dance in live performance.

Different body movements are directly linked with parameters of the music piece, such that fast movement should result in more complicated sound and vise versa, and higher head position corresponds to low/high pass filters. Also, for the two dancers in the piece, I designed the system to work in a way that they can move freely in the space covered by both 3D cameras and interact with each other.

This interactive system is originally made for an experimental dance performance in Brady's Bend limestone cave (more info). I collaborated with Evan Neely and Sara Adkins in making this system, where Evan composed electronic music which the system is interacting with.

This is where the piece was performed.
We were wearing DIY EL wire costumes while performing this piece in the limestone cave.
In the second stage of the research, I built a digital synthesizer to directly extract data from users to synthesize sound. It can detect the rhythm of users' movements and create beat. The background is a prototype of projection that also corresponds to users' movements.
